01 Project Context

CazéTV is building a digital prediction game (bolão) for the 2026 FIFA World Cup — a fan engagement platform where users make match predictions, earn points, compete in leagues, and receive prizes. More than 15 million users are expected to create accounts during the 2026 World Cup.

The engagement platform will be built on top of LiveLike. LiveLike does not store identity data (PII). It operates on a federated identity model: it receives an authentication token from our own SSO, creates an internal mapping (LiveLike ID ↔ CazéTV ID), and only stores gameplay data.

All user identity — registration, login, authentication — is exactly what we are looking to build with a vendor.

02 The Challenge

We need a vendor who takes full ownership of:

  1. Designing and implementing CazéTV's digital identity system and user database
  2. Operating and maintaining this infrastructure throughout the project lifecycle (World Cup 2026 and beyond)
  3. Guaranteeing SLA for availability and scalability, especially during extreme traffic spikes

We are talking about:

  • Mass B2C audience — Brazilian football fans with no technical background
  • Unpredictable registration spikes — millions of account creations within minutes during commercial announcements and platform launch
  • Aggressive delivery window — database must be live and integrated with LiveLike by end of April 2026
  • Future extensibility — architecture must support a sponsor as a second identity provider without full refactoring

04 Minimum Technical Requirements

We are not tied to any specific solution (Auth0, Firebase, Cognito, etc.). What matters is the outcome and contractual accountability.
  • Native support for OIDC / OAuth 2.0 / JWT — mandatory for LiveLike integration
  • Architecture on public cloud with auto-scaling configured
  • Proven capacity to handle peaks of 100k+ req/min for authentication
  • Compliance with Brazil's LGPD — PII stored in Brazil or with a proper data processing agreement
  • Extensible architecture for future identity federation (e.g., Sponsor as a secondary IdP)
